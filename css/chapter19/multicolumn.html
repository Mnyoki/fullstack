THE WINTER...S

play the play.

Now is the winter of our discontent Made glorious by this sun of York; And all the clouds that lour'd upon our house In the deep bosom of the ocean buried. Or bruised arms hung up for monuments; Our stern alarums changed to merry meetings, Our dreadful marches to delightful measures. grim-visaged war hath smooth'd his wrinkled front And now, instead of mounting barded steeds To fright the souls fearful adverseries, He capers nimbly in a lady's chamber To the lascivious pleasing of a lute
